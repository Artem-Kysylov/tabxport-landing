ChatGPT is excellent at generating structured data in table format, but its markdown output often contains formatting artifacts that can break your Excel imports or make your data look unprofessional. This guide will show you how to transform messy ChatGPT tables into clean, Excel-ready data in just a few steps.

## The Problem: ChatGPT's Markdown Artifacts

When ChatGPT generates tables, it uses markdown syntax that includes:

- **Pipe separators** (`|`) that become part of your data
- **Alignment markers** (`---`, `:---:`, `---:`) in the header row
- **Extra whitespace** around cells
- **Escaped characters** that don't render properly
- **Inconsistent formatting** across rows

Here's a typical example of what ChatGPT might output:

```markdown
| Product Name | Price | Stock | Category |
|--------------|-------|-------|----------|
| MacBook Pro  | $2,499| 15    | Computers|
| iPhone 14    |$999   | 32    |Phones    |
| AirPods Pro  | $249  |8      | Audio    |
```

This looks fine in markdown, but when you copy-paste it into Excel, you'll get formatting issues, extra columns, and inconsistent spacing.
